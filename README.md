# mibikemap

A single, open map of bike trails in Michigan gathered from official and community sources and sorted into one convenient map.

## Help grow the map 🚲

This is an open, community resource — and it's far from complete. If you know a
trail that's missing, maintain a local trail map, or spot something
mis-categorized, please pitch in:

- **Share a map:** a Google My Maps link, a GeoJSON file, or an ArcGIS /
  open-data layer can all be added as a new source.
- **Flag a fix:** tell us about trails that are missing, renamed, or in the
  wrong category.

[Open an issue on GitHub](https://github.com/brendensm/mibikemap/issues) — share
a link to your map or describe the fix, and we'll fold it in.

## Where the data comes from

Every segment keeps a link back to its source, which you'll find in its popup:

- **[MDNR Designated Biking Trails](https://gis-michigan.opendata.arcgis.com/datasets/4b7fc9bfa1224f1cbe753f16dc223629_3)**
  — The official statewide layer of biking trails designated by the Michigan
  Department of Natural Resources — state parks, forest pathways, and
  rail-trails.
- **[MDNR Statewide Rail-Trails](https://gis-michigan.opendata.arcgis.com/)** —
  The MDNR's statewide rail-trail inventory — every rail-trail in Michigan
  including county- and Friends-group-managed ones (Fred Meijer trails,
  Kal-Haven, Hart-Montague, Upper Peninsula grades), with surface types.
- **[Lansing Area Trails (community)](https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mid=1F_16PkqYRdjYzxvX_0o8KjRK1o3vmCw)**
  — A community-maintained Google map of Greater Lansing's paved trails, gravel
  trails, and on-street bike lanes.
- **[Bridge to Bay / Thumb (community)](https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mid=1rhTFlvNHUJtNVRcf46UCbJREvA2NL5Ij)**
  — A community Google map of the Bridge to Bay route and bikeways across
  Michigan's Thumb and Blue Water region.
- **[Traverse Area Regional Trail Network (community)](https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mid=1qW01G0whDPI8FmSpTFHk7qHVHTDLOCk)**
  — A community Google map of the TART trail network around Traverse City — the
  TART, Leelanau, Sleeping Bear Heritage and Boardman Lake trails, plus
  connectors.
- **[City of Midland Trails (city GIS)](https://www.cityofmidlandmi.gov/1386/Pere-Marquette-Rail-Trail)**
  — The City of Midland's public trails GIS — the Midland end of the Pere
  Marquette Rail-Trail plus the Chippewa Trail, Grand Curve Cycle Path, and City
  Forest mountain-bike singletrack.
- **[City of Midland Bike Routes (city GIS)](https://www.cityofmidlandmi.gov/204/Non-Motorized-Transportation)**
  — The City of Midland's planned non-motorized network — marked bike lanes,
  designated on-street bike routes, and paved-path connectors.
- **[Pere Marquette Rail-Trail (OpenStreetMap)](https://www.openstreetmap.org/search?query=Pere%20Marquette%20Rail-Trail)**
  — The full Pere Marquette corridor from OpenStreetMap: the Rail-Trail
  (Midland–Clare) continuing as the State Trail (Clare–Baldwin).
- **[Lower-Peninsula rail-trails (OpenStreetMap)](https://www.openstreetmap.org/)**
  — Smaller Lower-Peninsula rail-trails not in the state layers — e.g. Genesee
  Valley, Baw Beese, Alpena–Hillman, the Detroit Dequindre Cut — from
  OpenStreetMap.
- **[Lapeer Linear Park Pathway (Outdoor Michigan)](https://outdoormichigan.org/feature/14751)**
  — The Lapeer Linear Park Pathway, from the Outdoor Michigan trail guide.

Background maps © OpenStreetMap contributors and CARTO.
